    What Most Children Do at 12 months

    Social/Emotional

  1. Is shy or nervous with strangers
  2. Cries when mom or dad leaves
  3. Has favorite things and people
  4. Shows fear in some situations
  5. Hands you a book when he wants to hear a story
  6. Repeats sounds or actions to get attention
  7. Puts out arm or leg to help with dressing
  8. Plays games such as “peek-a-boo” and “pat-a-cake”
    9. Language/Communication

  9. Uses simple gestures, like shaking head “no” or waving “bye-bye”
  10. Responds to simple spoken requests
  11. Makes sounds with changes in tone (sounds more like speech)
  12. Says “mama” and “dada” and exclamations like “uh-oh!”
  13. Tries to say words you say
    14. Cognitive (learning, thinking, problem-solving)

  14. Explores things in different ways, like shaking, banging, throwing
  15. Finds hidden things easily
  16. Looks at the right picture or thing when it’s named
  17. Copies gestures
  18. Starts to use things correctly; for example, drinks from a cup, brushes hair
  19. Bangs two things together
  20. Puts things in a container, takes things out of a container
  21. Lets things go without help
  22. Pokes with index (pointer) finger
  23. Follows simple directions like “pick up the toy”
    24. Learn about 18 months (CDC.gov)
